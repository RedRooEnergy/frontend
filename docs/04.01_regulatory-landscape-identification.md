04.01 — Regulatory Landscape Identification
1. Purpose

This document identifies and categorises all regulatory, statutory, and standards-based regimes that apply to the marketplace, its operators, suppliers, buyers, and transactions.

Its purpose is to:

• Prevent regulatory blind spots
• Define the compliance surface area
• Establish jurisdictional assumptions
• Provide the authoritative source for compliance mapping
• Serve as the upstream reference for the Compliance Matrix

This document does not define controls.
It defines what must be complied with.

2. Regulatory Scope Assumptions

Unless explicitly superseded by future jurisdictional expansion, the platform assumes:

• Marketplace operator jurisdiction: Australia
• Initial buyer jurisdiction: Australia
• Initial supplier jurisdiction: International (including China)
• Cross-border importation of regulated physical goods
• Ongoing regulator visibility

3. Regulatory Categories

All applicable regulation is grouped into the following domains:

Corporate & Commercial Law

Consumer Protection & Fair Trading

Electrical & Energy Product Regulation

Import, Customs & Border Control

Taxation & Financial Reporting

Payments & Financial Services

Data Protection & Privacy

Cybersecurity & System Integrity

Advertising & Marketing Conduct

Dispute Resolution & Litigation

4. Corporate & Commercial Law

Applicable regimes include:

• Corporations Act (Australia)
• Contract law (formation, enforceability)
• Agency and intermediary obligations
• Director and officer duties
• Insolvency and continuity considerations

5. Consumer Protection & Fair Trading

Applicable regimes include:

• Australian Consumer Law (ACL)
• Misleading or deceptive conduct prohibitions
• Consumer guarantees
• Unfair contract terms
• Refund and remedy obligations

6. Electrical & Energy Product Regulation

Applicable regimes include (non-exhaustive):

• Electrical safety legislation
• Energy efficiency schemes
• Mandatory product certifications
• Market access restrictions
• Product recall obligations

Products cannot be sold unless compliant.

7. Import, Customs & Border Control

Applicable regimes include:

• Australian Border Force requirements
• Customs Act and regulations
• HS classification obligations
• Duty and tariff compliance
• Importer of record responsibilities

8. Taxation & Financial Reporting

Applicable regimes include:

• GST obligations
• Tax invoice requirements
• Withholding considerations
• Record retention rules
• Audit-readiness requirements

9. Payments & Financial Services

Applicable regimes include:

• Payment services regulations
• Anti-money laundering (AML) expectations
• Know Your Customer (KYC) obligations
• Escrow and settlement rules
• Chargeback and dispute handling

10. Data Protection & Privacy

Applicable regimes include:

• Australian Privacy Act
• Cross-border data transfer rules
• Consent and disclosure requirements
• Data retention and destruction obligations
• Breach notification requirements

11. Cybersecurity & System Integrity

Applicable regimes include:

• Reasonable security expectations
• Incident reporting obligations
• Auditability requirements
• System reliability expectations

12. Advertising & Marketing Conduct

Applicable regimes include:

• Truth in advertising laws
• Claims substantiation requirements
• Prohibited representations
• Influencer and promotion disclosures

13. Dispute Resolution & Litigation

Applicable regimes include:

• Court jurisdiction rules
• Alternative dispute resolution frameworks
• Evidence preservation requirements
• Subpoena and disclosure obligations

14. Regulatory Authority Interfaces

The platform must be capable of interacting with:

• Consumer regulators
• Electrical safety regulators
• Customs and border agencies
• Tax authorities
• Courts and tribunals

15. Exclusions

This document explicitly excludes:

• Internal platform controls
• Technical enforcement mechanisms
• Compliance workflows

Those are defined in subsequent Phase 4 documents.
